Charts in Power BI

•Comparing Categories:
 Bar Chart, Column Chart. (Best for: Sales by Product, Count by Category).

•Showing Trend Over Time: 
Line Chart, Area Chart. (Best for: Sales over several months or years).

•Showing Part-of-a-Whole: 
Pie Chart, Doughnut Chart, Treemap. (Best for: Market Share, Budget Allocation).

•Showing Correlation:
 Scatter Chart. (Best for: Seeing if two things are related, like Marketing Spend vs. Sales).

•Showing Cumulative Effect: 
Waterfall Chart. (Best for: Profit & Loss statements, understanding how a starting value reaches an end value).

•Showing a Process/Funnel: 
Funnel Chart. (Best for: Sales pipeline, conversion rates).

•Geographic Data: 
Map, Filled Map. (Best for: Sales by Country or Region).

•Showing a Single Metric: 
Gauge, Card. (Best for: KPI like Total Revenue, Current Stock Price).

•Showing Tabular Data: 
Table, Matrix. (A Matrix is like a pivot table; it can group and summarize data).

------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Power BI Chart Types & Their Uses(ONE LINER)

Bar / Column Chart:  Compare values across categories.
Line:                Show trends over time.
Pie:                 Show parts of a whole (few categories).
Donut:               Like a pie chart with a center value.
Treemap:             Part-to-whole with many categories.
Stacked:             Category & subcategory comparison.
Area:                Cumulative trends over time.
Table:               Show detailed raw data.
Matrix:              Like pivot table of excel.
Card:                Show single key metric (e.g., total sales).
Scatter:             Correlation between two numeric variables.
Waterfall:           Cumulative totals with addition & subtraction.
Gauge:               Progress towards goal (visual speedometer).
Combo Chart:         Compare two measures with different scales.
KPI:                 Focused measure showing current vs. target.
Q&A:                 Natural language queries driving visuals.
